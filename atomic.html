Atomic Habits



Atomic Habits by James Clear is a practical guide to building good habits, breaking bad ones, and mastering the tiny behaviors that lead to remarkable results. The central idea is that small, consistent changes—atomic habits—compound over time to create significant improvements. Clear outlines a four-step model of habit formation: Cue, Craving, Response, and Reward, and provides a framework called the Four Laws of Behavior Change to shape habits effectively: Make it Obvious, Make it Attractive, Make it Easy, and Make it Satisfying. He emphasizes the power of focusing on systems rather than goals and encourages readers to build an identity around the kind of person they want to become. By aligning daily actions with long-term aspirations, anyone can transform their habits and, ultimately, their life.
